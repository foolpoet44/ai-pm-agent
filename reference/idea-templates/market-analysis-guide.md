# Market Analysis Guide

## 1. Market Sizing

### 1.1 TAM (Total Addressable Market)
**Definition:** Total market demand for a product or service
**Calculation Method:**
- Top-down: Industry reports, market research
- Bottom-up: Unit economics × potential customers
- Value theory: Estimated value to customers

**Data Sources:**
- Gartner, Forrester, IDC reports
- Government statistics
- Industry associations
- Academic research

### 1.2 SAM (Serviceable Available Market)
**Definition:** Portion of TAM you can reach with your product/service
**Calculation:**
- TAM × (Your target segment / Total market)
- Geographic constraints
- Product limitations
- Regulatory restrictions

### 1.3 SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market)
**Definition:** Portion of SAM you can realistically capture
**Calculation:**
- SAM × Expected market share (1-5 years)
- Consider competition
- Resource constraints
- Go-to-market strategy

**Example:**
```
TAM: $10B (Global productivity software market)
SAM: $2B (Small business productivity tools in US)
SOM: $100M (5% market share in 3 years)
```

## 2. Competitive Analysis

### 2.1 Direct Competitors
**Identification:**
- Companies solving the same problem
- Similar product features
- Same target audience

**Analysis Framework:**
| Competitor | Strengths | Weaknesses | Market Share | Pricing |
|------------|-----------|------------|--------------|---------|
| Competitor A | | | | |
| Competitor B | | | | |

### 2.2 Indirect Competitors
**Identification:**
- Alternative solutions to the same problem
- Different approach but same outcome
- Substitute products/services

### 2.3 Competitive Positioning
**Differentiation Strategy:**
- Cost Leadership: Lowest price
- Differentiation: Unique features/quality
- Focus: Niche market specialization

**Positioning Map:**
```
High Price
    |
    |   [Competitor A]
    |
    |           [Your Product]
    |
    |   [Competitor B]
    |
Low Price -------------------- High Quality
    |
    |   [Competitor C]
    |
Low Quality
```

## 3. Market Trends

### 3.1 Industry Trends
**Analysis Areas:**
- Technology trends
- Consumer behavior shifts
- Regulatory changes
- Economic factors

**PESTLE Analysis:**
- **P**olitical: Government policies, regulations
- **E**conomic: GDP growth, inflation, unemployment
- **S**ocial: Demographics, lifestyle changes
- **T**echnological: Innovation, automation
- **L**egal: Laws, compliance requirements
- **E**nvironmental: Sustainability, climate change

### 3.2 Growth Opportunities
**Market Gaps:**
- Underserved segments
- Emerging needs
- Technology enablers
- Geographic expansion

### 3.3 Threats & Risks
**Risk Assessment:**
- New entrants
- Substitute products
- Changing customer preferences
- Technology disruption

### 4.2 Customer Journey
**Stages:**
1. **Awareness:** How do they discover the problem?
2. **Consideration:** What solutions do they evaluate?
3. **Decision:** What drives purchase decision?
4. **Retention:** What keeps them using the product?
5. **Advocacy:** What makes them recommend it?

### 4.3 Jobs-to-be-Done Framework
**Template:**
```
When I [situation],
I want to [motivation],
So I can [expected outcome].

Example:
When I'm working on multiple projects,
I want to organize my tasks automatically,
So I can focus on important work without feeling overwhelmed.
```
